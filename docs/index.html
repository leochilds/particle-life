Particle Life

Thousands of particles in a few colors drift on a wraparound world. Every ordered pair of colors has a single number — how strongly one is attracted to (or repelled by) the other. That little matrix is the entire rule.

No genomes, no networks, no goals. Yet what emerges looks alive: cells with membranes, predators and prey, wandering organisms, structures that heal when torn. The complexity lives in the interactions, not in any design.

Most random universes are dull, so the vitality meter scores each one on clustering, motion and change. When a universe goes quiet you're nudged to spawn a fresh one — or let auto-replace do it.

  • Drag the canvas to stir · right-drag / shift-drag to repel
  • space pause · n new universe · h hide the UI
  • Share copies a link that recreates the exact universe
